CV (last updated: November 26th, 2025)

Vasilis Livanos

Post-doctoral researcher at CMM.
 

Research interests:
Algorithmic Game Theory, Algorithms under Uncertainty,
Combinatorial Optimization, Online Mechanism Design.

I am a post-doctoral researcher at CMM, hosted by the fantastic duo of José Correa and José Soto. My research interests lie in algorithmic game theory, with a focus on theoretical problems related to the theme of 'optimization under uncertainty' and mechanism design.

Previously, I was a visiting post-doctoral researcher at EPFL, where I was hosted by the amazing Andrés Cristi. Prior to that, I was a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Chile, where I was a member of the Information and Computation in Market Design (ICMD) team. Before that, I was a student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), where I was extremely fortunate to be co-advised by Ruta Mehta and Chandra Chekuri.

Before joining the theory group at UIUC, I received my Diploma in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens where I was a member of the Computation and Reasoning Lab and I was lucky enough to be advised by Dimitris Fotakis.
